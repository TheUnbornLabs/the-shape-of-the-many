# References — 25. The collective intelligence factor "c"

## Confirmed sources

- Woolley, A. W., Chabris, C. F., Pentland, A., Hashmi, N., & Malone, T. W. "Evidence for a Collective Intelligence Factor in the Performance of Human Groups." *Science*, vol. 330, no. 6004, 29 October 2010, pp. 686–688 (DOI 10.1126/science.1193147; PMID 20929725). — The founding study: extracts a single general factor *c* from group performance across a task battery; reports that *c* is only weakly related to members' average and maximum individual intelligence, and correlates instead with average social sensitivity (measured via "Reading the Mind in the Eyes"), evenness of conversational turn-taking, and the proportion of women (largely mediated by social sensitivity).

- Spearman, C. "'General Intelligence,' Objectively Determined and Measured." *American Journal of Psychology*, 1904. — The origin of the individual general-intelligence factor *g*, the deliberate psychometric analogue on which the *c* argument is modelled.

- Engel, D., Woolley, A. W., Jing, L. X., Chabris, C. F., & Malone, T. W. "Reading the Mind in the Eyes or Reading Between the Lines? Theory of Mind Predicts Collective Intelligence Equally Well Online and Face-to-Face." *PLOS ONE*, vol. 9, no. 12, December 2014, e115212 (DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0115212; PMID 25514387). — Finds evidence for a collective-intelligence factor in both online and face-to-face groups, supporting that *c* is not merely an artifact of physical co-presence.

- Baron-Cohen, S., Wheelwright, S., Hill, J., Raste, Y., & Plumb, I. "The 'Reading the Mind in the Eyes' Test Revised Version: A Study with Normal Adults, and Adults with Asperger Syndrome or High-Functioning Autism." *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry*, vol. 42, no. 2, 2001, pp. 241–251. — The social-sensitivity instrument used to measure the strongest correlate of *c*; its construct validity is itself contested (see critique).

- Woodley, Michael A., and Edward Bell. "Is Collective Intelligence (Mostly) the General Factor of Personality? A Comment on Woolley, Chabris, Pentland, Hashmi and Malone (2010)." *Intelligence*, vol. 39, no. 2–3, 2011, pp. 79–81 (DOI 10.1016/j.intell.2011.01.004). — The critique-beat comment arguing that *c* may be, mostly, a group-level manifestation of the general factor of personality; underpins the critique beat's "interpretation" objection.
