# References — 6. Phase transitions & thresholds

## Confirmed sources

- Broadbent, S. R. and Hammersley, J. M. "Percolation Processes. I. Crystals and Mazes." *Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society*, 53, 629–641, 1957. — The founding paper of percolation theory; the source for the threshold at which a system-spanning connected cluster appears abruptly.
- Stauffer, Dietrich (and Ammon Aharony in later editions). *Introduction to Percolation Theory*. Taylor & Francis, 1985 (2nd ed. 1992). — Standard accessible treatment of percolation thresholds and giant clusters; supports the forest-fire anchor example.
- Ising, Ernst. "Beitrag zur Theorie des Ferromagnetismus." *Zeitschrift für Physik*, 31, 253–258, 1925. — Ising's solution of the one-dimensional model (his 1925 Hamburg dissertation), which found no transition in 1D; supports the origin narrative.
- Niss, Martin. "History of the Lenz–Ising Model 1920–1950: From Ferromagnetic to Cooperative Phenomena." *Archive for History of Exact Sciences*, 59, 267–318, 2005. — Historical source confirming Lenz's 1920 proposal, Ising's 1925 dissertation, and the model's later development.
- Onsager, Lars. "Crystal Statistics. I. A Two-Dimensional Model with an Order-Disorder Transition." *Physical Review*, 65, 117–149, 1944. — The exact solution of the two-dimensional Ising model demonstrating a genuine phase transition.
- Peierls, Rudolf. "On Ising's Model of Ferromagnetism." *Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society*, 32, 477–481, 1936. — Established that the two-dimensional Ising model undergoes a phase transition, correcting Ising's assumption.
- Stanley, H. Eugene. *Introduction to Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena*. Oxford University Press, 1971. — Standard reference for critical phenomena, order parameters, and the divergence of the correlation length at criticality.
- Granovetter, Mark. "Threshold Models of Collective Behavior." *American Journal of Sociology*, 83(6), 1420–1443, 1978. — The social threshold model cross-referenced as item 7; supports the claim that collective action can flip when a distribution of individual thresholds chains.
