# References — 15. Kin selection & inclusive fitness

## Confirmed sources

- Hamilton, W. D. "The Genetical Evolution of Social Behaviour, I and II." *Journal of Theoretical Biology*, vol. 7, no. 1, 1964, pp. 1–52. — The founding papers; introduces inclusive fitness and the condition later known as Hamilton's rule (rb > c). Also the source of the haplodiploidy argument for eusociality.
- Maynard Smith, J. "Group Selection and Kin Selection." *Nature*, vol. 201, 1964, pp. 1145–1147. — Coins the term "kin selection."
- Sherman, P. W. "Nepotism and the Evolution of Alarm Calls." *Science*, vol. 197, no. 4310, 1977, pp. 1246–1253. — The Belding's ground squirrel study: females (who remain near kin) give alarm calls far more than dispersing males; calling tracks presence of relatives. The chapter's anchor example.
- Dawkins, R. *The Selfish Gene*. Oxford University Press, 1976. — Popularised the gene's-eye view of evolution and inclusive-fitness thinking for a general audience.
- Price, G. R. "Selection and Covariance." *Nature*, vol. 227, 1970, pp. 520–521. — The covariance (Price) equation, which recasts selection statistically and yields Hamilton's rule as a special case.
- Nowak, M. A., Tarnita, C. E., & Wilson, E. O. "The Evolution of Eusociality." *Nature*, vol. 466, 2010, pp. 1057–1062. — The high-profile attack on the generality of inclusive-fitness theory.
- Abbot, P., et al. "Inclusive Fitness Theory and Eusociality." *Nature*, vol. 471, 2011, E1–E4. — The rebuttal signed by 137 authors (Abbot plus 136 co-signatories) defending inclusive fitness. (Verified: 137 authors.)
