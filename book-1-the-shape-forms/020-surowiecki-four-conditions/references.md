# References — 20. Surowiecki's four conditions

## Confirmed sources

- Surowiecki, James. *The Wisdom of Crowds: Why the Many Are Smarter than the Few and How Collective Wisdom Shapes Business, Economies, Societies, and Nations*. Doubleday, 2004. — The primary source. Origin of the four-conditions framing (diversity, independence, decentralisation, aggregation); the argument that removing any one degrades the crowd toward folly; the opening use of Galton's fair; and the market examples used throughout beats 1–4.
- Galton, Francis. "Vox Populi." *Nature*, vol. 75, 1907, pp. 450–451. — The averaged ox-weight estimate; the founding demonstration of aggregation that Surowiecki opens with (cross-referenced as item 17).
- Condorcet, Marie Jean Antoine Nicolas de Caritat, Marquis de. *Essai sur l'application de l'analyse à la probabilité des décisions rendues à la pluralité des voix*. Paris, 1785. — The mathematics of independent-voter aggregation underpinning the independence and aggregation conditions (cross-referenced as item 18).
- Hayek, Friedrich A. "The Use of Knowledge in Society." *American Economic Review*, vol. 35, no. 4, 1945, pp. 519–530. — The argument that a price system marshals dispersed, local knowledge no central planner could assemble; the intellectual root of Surowiecki's decentralisation condition.
- Page, Scott E. *The Difference: How the Power of Diversity Creates Better Groups, Firms, Schools, and Societies*. Princeton University Press, 2007. — The formal treatment of diversity subtracting shared error, underpinning the diversity condition (cross-referenced as item 19).
