# References — 4. Stigmergy

## Confirmed sources

- Grassé, Pierre-Paul. "La reconstruction du nid et les coordinations interindividuelles chez *Bellicositermes natalensis* et *Cubitermes* sp. La théorie de la stigmergie: Essai d'interprétation du comportement des termites constructeurs." *Insectes Sociaux*, vol. 6, no. 1, March 1959, pp. 41–80. DOI 10.1007/BF02223791. — The founding paper; coins "stigmergie" from termite nest-building. Supports the origin beat and the *stigma* + *ergon* etymology. VERIFIED against the publisher of record (Springer/Insectes Sociaux); page range corrected from the previously stated 41–83 to 41–80.

- Deneubourg, J.-L., Aron, S., Goss, S., and Pasteels, J.-M. "The self-organizing exploratory pattern of the Argentine ant." *Journal of Insect Behavior*, vol. 3, 1990, pp. 159–168. — Double-bridge / trail-formation work at the Université Libre de Bruxelles; supports the anchor example and the shortest-path-via-pheromone mechanism.

- Goss, S., Aron, S., Deneubourg, J.-L., and Pasteels, J.-M. "Self-organized shortcuts in the Argentine ant." *Naturwissenschaften*, vol. 76, 1989, pp. 579–581. — The classic double-bridge shortest-path result; core support for the anchor example.

- Bonabeau, Eric, Dorigo, Marco, and Theraulaz, Guy. *Swarm Intelligence: From Natural to Artificial Systems.* Oxford University Press, 1999. — Standard synthesis linking stigmergy to ant-colony optimisation; supports the mechanism, the swarm-intelligence cross-reference (item 86), and the quantitative/qualitative distinction.

- Theraulaz, Guy, and Bonabeau, Eric. "A brief history of stigmergy." *Artificial Life*, vol. 5, no. 2, 1999, pp. 97–116. — Review tracing the concept from Grassé forward; supports the origin, the quantitative vs. qualitative stigmergy distinction, and the positive/negative-feedback framing.

- Canonical double-bridge experiment. — VERIFIED: the classic shortest-path result is Goss, Aron, Deneubourg & Pasteels, "Self-organized shortcuts in the Argentine ant," *Naturwissenschaften* 76: 579–581 (1989), using the Argentine ant (*Linepithema humilis*, formerly *Iridomyrmex/Linepithema humilis*), with the accompanying probabilistic model. The chapter's attribution to "Deneubourg and Goss," "late 1980s," and "Argentine ants" is accurate. (Precise branch-length ratio and exact convergence time are reported in the paper; the chapter states "often under an hour," which is consistent, and otherwise avoids asserting a specific ratio.)
- Grassé 1959 title and page range. — VERIFIED against the journal of record (Insectes Sociaux 6:1, pp. 41–80, DOI 10.1007/BF02223791); page range corrected in the Confirmed sources entry above.
- Quantitative vs. qualitative stigmergy distinction. — VERIFIED as set out in Theraulaz & Bonabeau, "A Brief History of Stigmergy," *Artificial Life* 5(2): 97–116 (1999), which explicitly treats the two distinct stigmergic mechanisms.
