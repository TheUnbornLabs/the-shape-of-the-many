# References — 8. Complex adaptive systems

## Confirmed sources

- Holland, John H. *Hidden Order: How Adaptation Builds Complexity*. Addison-Wesley, 1995. — The core anatomy of a CAS: agents, tags, internal models, building blocks, and adaptation; primary source for the mechanism beat.
- Holland, John H. *Emergence: From Chaos to Order*. Addison-Wesley, 1998. — Holland's account of how emergent macro-order arises from local rules; supports the idea and mechanism beats.
- Weaver, Warren. "Science and Complexity." *American Scientist*, vol. 36, no. 4 (1948), pp. 536–544. — The founding distinction between problems of simplicity, disorganised complexity, and organised complexity; underpins the origin beat and the complex-vs-complicated distinction.
- Waldrop, M. Mitchell. *Complexity: The Emerging Science at the Edge of Order and Chaos*. Simon & Schuster, 1992. — Narrative history of the Santa Fe Institute and its founders (Gell-Mann, Arthur, Kauffman, Holland); supports the origin beat.
- Mitchell, Melanie. *Complexity: A Guided Tour*. Oxford University Press, 2009. — Insider survey of complexity science; source for the critique beat's honest assessment of the field's predictive limits.

## Verified during citation pass

- Warren Weaver, "Science and Complexity," *American Scientist* 36(4) (October 1948), pp. 536–544 — confirmed; source of the simplicity / disorganised-complexity / organised-complexity distinction.
- Santa Fe Institute founded 1984 — confirmed. Founders include George Cowan, David Pines, Stirling Colgate, Murray Gell-Mann, Nick Metropolis, Herb Anderson, Peter Carruthers, and Richard Slansky; most were associated with Los Alamos National Laboratory. Incorporated May 1984 (initially the Rio Grande Institute); launch workshops "Emerging Syntheses in Science" held October–November 1984.
- Murray Gell-Mann's Nobel Prize in Physics 1969 — confirmed; the official citation reads "for his contributions and discoveries concerning the classification of elementary particles and their interactions." (Chapter prose corrected from "won the Nobel for the quark" to reflect this citation, since the 1969 prize was not awarded specifically for quarks.)
- John Holland invented genetic algorithms; primary source *Adaptation in Natural and Artificial Systems* (University of Michigan Press, 1975). Affiliations University of Michigan and the Santa Fe Institute — confirmed.
- Holland, *Hidden Order* (Addison-Wesley, 1995) and *Emergence: From Chaos to Order* (Addison-Wesley, 1998; Oxford UP edition also 1998) — confirmed.
- Warren Weaver co-authored *The Mathematical Theory of Communication* (University of Illinois Press, 1949) with Claude Shannon — confirmed.
