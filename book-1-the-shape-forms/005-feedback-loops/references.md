# References — 5. Positive & negative feedback loops

## Confirmed sources

- Norbert Wiener. *Cybernetics: or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine*. MIT Press / Hermann & Cie, 1948. — Founding text of cybernetics; source for the *kybernetes* / steersman etymology and the unification of feedback across machines and organisms.
- James Clerk Maxwell. "On Governors." *Proceedings of the Royal Society of London*, vol. 16, 1867–1868, pp. 270–283. DOI 10.1098/rspl.1867.0055. — First rigorous mathematical analysis of feedback-regulated engines and the conditions for stability versus growing oscillation; foundational to control theory. VERIFIED (journal, volume, pages; read Mar. 5, 1868). Note: Wiener himself later credited this as the first significant paper on feedback mechanisms.
- Jay W. Forrester. *Industrial Dynamics*. MIT Press, Cambridge, Mass., 1961. — Extension of coupled positive/negative feedback loops (with delays) to firms, supply chains, and economies; origin of system dynamics. VERIFIED.
- James Watt's centrifugal (flyball) governor. — VERIFIED: Watt adapted and patented the centrifugal governor for the steam engine in 1788 (the flyball mechanism itself predates him in windmill/millstone use). The chapter's "late eighteenth century" framing and negative-feedback characterisation are accurate.
