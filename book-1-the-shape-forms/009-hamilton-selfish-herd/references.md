# References — 9. Hamilton's selfish herd

## Confirmed sources

- Hamilton, W. D. "Geometry for the Selfish Herd." *Journal of Theoretical Biology*, vol. 31, no. 2, 1971, pp. 295–311. — The founding paper. Source of the concept, the domain of danger, the ring-of-frogs / surfacing-snake one-dimensional model, the two-dimensional polygon (Voronoi/Dirichlet) treatment, and the argument that aggregation arises from individual selfishness without group selection.

- Wynne-Edwards, V. C. *Animal Dispersion in Relation to Social Behaviour.* Oliver & Boyd, 1962. — The influential statement of "for the good of the group/species" group selection that Hamilton's selfish herd (and his generation more broadly) argued against; supports the origin beat's account of the intellectual target.

- Viscido, S. V.; Miller, M.; Wethey, D. S. "The dilemma of the selfish herd: the search for a realistic movement rule." *Journal of Theoretical Biology*, vol. 217, no. 2, 2002, pp. 183–194. — Simulation-based test of which movement rules actually reduce an individual's domain of danger; finds the simplest nearest-neighbour rule weak and motivates the critique beat's "empirical record is mixed" claim.
