# References — 22. The Delphi method

## Confirmed sources

- Dalkey, Norman, and Olaf Helmer. "An Experimental Application of the Delphi Method to the Use of Experts." *Management Science* 9, no. 3 (1963): 458–467. — The classic public statement of the method; reports controlled trials of anonymous, iterative expert polling and its convergence behaviour. Underpins the origin and mechanism beats.
- Linstone, Harold A., and Murray Turoff, eds. *The Delphi Method: Techniques and Applications.* Reading, MA: Addison-Wesley, 1975. — The standard reference volume collecting the technique's methodology and its spread across forecasting, policy, and industry. Underpins the origin beat and the account of Delphi's proliferation.
- Rowe, Gene, and George Wright. "The Delphi Technique as a Forecasting Tool: Issues and Analysis." *International Journal of Forecasting* 15, no. 4 (1999): 353–375. — Critical review of the Delphi literature; finds Delphi generally outperforms unstructured interacting groups but that evidence of superiority over simple averaging or well-run interacting groups is weak and mixed. Underpins the critique beat.
- Surowiecki, James. *The Wisdom of Crowds.* New York: Doubleday, 2004. — Source of the four-conditions framing (diversity, independence, decentralisation, aggregation) that beat 5 argues Delphi engineers on purpose. Cross-referenced as item 20.
- Condorcet, Marquis de. *Essai sur l'application de l'analyse à la probabilité des décisions rendues à la pluralité des voix.* Paris, 1785. — The aggregation-of-independent-judgments floor invoked in the critique (item 18).
