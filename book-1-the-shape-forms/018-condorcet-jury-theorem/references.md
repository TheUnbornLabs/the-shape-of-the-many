# References — 18. Condorcet's Jury Theorem

## Confirmed sources

- Condorcet, Marquis de (Marie Jean Antoine Nicolas de Caritat). *Essai sur l'application de l'analyse à la probabilité des décisions rendues à la pluralité des voix.* Paris: Imprimerie Royale, 1785. — The original statement of the theorem. Its Discours préliminaire opens by dedicating the work to the memory of Turgot ("Un grand homme, dont je regretterai toujours les leçons, les exemples, et surtout l'amitié"), Condorcet's mentor and patron, confirming the Turgot framing. Digitised copy available via the Bibliothèque nationale de France (Gallica, ark:/12148/bpt6k417181).
- Boland, Philip J. "Majority Systems and the Condorcet Jury Theorem." *The Statistician* (Journal of the Royal Statistical Society, Series D), vol. 38, no. 3, 1989, pp. 181–189. — A clear modern statement and generalisation; source for the homogeneous-competence and majority-system analysis.
- "Condorcet's jury theorem." *Encyclopaedia / reference overview.* — Used to confirm the two-edged result (p > 1/2 → 1; p < 1/2 → 0 as n → ∞), the independence and homogeneity assumptions, and Condorcet's dates (1743–1794). Cross-checked against the sources above.
- Biographical facts on Condorcet (1743–1794; philosophe; death during the Terror, 1794) are uncontested and drawn from standard reference biographies.
