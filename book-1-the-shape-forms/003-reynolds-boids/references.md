# References — 3. Reynolds' boids

## Confirmed sources

- Reynolds, Craig W. "Flocks, Herds, and Schools: A Distributed Behavioral Model." *Computer Graphics* (SIGGRAPH '87 Conference Proceedings), 21(4), 25–34, 1987. — The founding paper; source for the three rules (separation, alignment, cohesion), the term "boid," the local-perception / no-leader architecture, and the computer-graphics origin.
- Vicsek, Tamás; Czirók, András; Ben-Jacob, Eshel; Cohen, Inon; Shochet, Ofer. "Novel Type of Phase Transition in a System of Self-Driven Particles." *Physical Review Letters*, 75(6), 1226–1229, 1995. — The statistical-physics formalisation of collective motion (item 99); source for the density-driven phase transition from disordered to ordered motion.
- Ballerini, M.; Cabibbo, N.; Candelier, R.; Cavagna, A.; Giardina, I.; et al. "Interaction ruling animal collective behavior depends on topological rather than metric distance: Evidence from a field study." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)*, 105(4), 1232–1237, 2008. — The STARFLAG Rome starling study; source for the topological-neighbour finding (~6–7 neighbours tracked by rank, not radius) that refined Reynolds.
- Hamilton, W. D. "Geometry for the Selfish Herd." *Journal of Theoretical Biology*, 31(2), 295–311, 1971. — Evolutionary basis for cohesion (item 9): aggregation as reduction of individual predation risk.

- Helbing, Dirk; Farkas, Illés; Vicsek, Tamás. "Simulating dynamical features of escape panic." *Nature*, 407, 487–490, 2000. DOI 10.1038/35035023. — Pedestrian-flow self-organisation (lane formation) and the physics of crowd pressure/escape panic; supports the human-weight beat's account of local rules producing graceful flow or lethal crush. VERIFIED.
- *Batman Returns* (1992, dir. Tim Burton) — VERIFIED that Reynolds-derived boids flocking was used for the bat swarms and penguin "army": modified versions of Reynolds' original boids software (developed at Symbolics) were used, with bat-swarm work by Andy Kopra (VIFX) and penguin animation by Boss Films. (Reynolds received a Scientific and Engineering Academy Award in 1998.)
