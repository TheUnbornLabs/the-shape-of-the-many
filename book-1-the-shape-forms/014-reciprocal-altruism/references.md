# References — 14. Reciprocal altruism

## Confirmed sources

- Trivers, R. L. "The Evolution of Reciprocal Altruism." *The Quarterly Review of Biology*, vol. 46, no. 1, 1971, pp. 35–57. — The founding paper; sets out cost-benefit logic of delayed-repayment altruism between non-kin, with the human, cleaning-fish, and warning-call cases.
- Axelrod, R., and Hamilton, W. D. "The Evolution of Cooperation." *Science*, vol. 211, no. 4489, 1981, pp. 1390–1396. — Formalises reciprocal altruism via the iterated Prisoner's Dilemma; TIT-FOR-TAT and evolutionary stability of reciprocators.
- Axelrod, R. *The Evolution of Cooperation*. Basic Books, 1984. — Book-length account of the computer tournaments; source for the shadow of the future and the "nice, provocable, forgiving" characterisation of TIT-FOR-TAT.
- Wilkinson, G. S. "Reciprocal food sharing in the vampire bat." *Nature*, vol. 308, 1984, pp. 181–184. — The anchor example: regurgitated blood-sharing in *Desmodus rotundus*, sharing conditioned on prior partners, favourable donor-cost/recipient-benefit ratio.
- Nowak, M. A., and Sigmund, K. "Evolution of indirect reciprocity by image scoring." *Nature*, vol. 393, 1998, pp. 573–577. — Foundational model of indirect reciprocity (help those with good reputations).
- Nowak, M. A., and Sigmund, K. "Evolution of indirect reciprocity." *Nature*, vol. 437, 2005, pp. 1291–1298. — Review consolidating direct vs indirect reciprocity and the role of reputation.
