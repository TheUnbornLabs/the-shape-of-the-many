# References — 2. Self-organisation

## Confirmed sources

- Prigogine, Ilya, and Isabelle Stengers. *Order Out of Chaos: Man's New Dialogue with Nature*. Bantam, 1984. — The accessible statement of dissipative structures and far-from-equilibrium order; underpins the origin and mechanism beats.
- Nicolis, Grégoire, and Ilya Prigogine. *Self-Organization in Nonequilibrium Systems*. Wiley, 1977. — Technical foundation of dissipative-structure theory; supports the thermodynamic framing and the Nobel-era work.
- Haken, Hermann. *Synergetics: An Introduction*. Springer, 1977. — Founds synergetics and the laser as a model of spontaneous collective order; supports the origin beat.
- Camazine, Scott, Jean-Louis Deneubourg, Nigel R. Franks, James Sneyd, Guy Theraulaz, and Eric Bonabeau. *Self-Organization in Biological Systems*. Princeton University Press, 2001. — The standard rigorous reference; supports the mechanism, termite/stigmergy anchor, and the critique of hand-waviness.
- Kauffman, Stuart A. *The Origins of Order: Self-Organization and Selection in Evolution*. Oxford University Press, 1993. — Source of "order for free"; supports the mechanism beat.
- Kauffman, Stuart A. *At Home in the Universe: The Search for the Laws of Self-Organization and Complexity*. Oxford University Press, 1995. — Popular statement of "order for free."
- Seeley, Thomas D. *Honeybee Democracy*. Princeton University Press, 2010. — Leaderless near-optimal nest-site selection; underpins the flip beat (cross-ref item 68).
- Hayek, Friedrich A. *Law, Legislation and Liberty, Volume 1: Rules and Order*. University of Chicago Press, 1973. — Spontaneous order as market, law, and language; underpins the human-weight beat.
- Hayek, Friedrich A. "The Use of Knowledge in Society." *American Economic Review*, 35(4), 1945. — The price system as decentralised information processing; supports the human-weight beat.
- Ashby, W. Ross. "Principles of the Self-Organizing System." In *Principles of Self-Organization*, ed. Von Foerster and Zopf, Pergamon, 1962. — Early cybernetic framing of self-organisation; supports the origin beat.

- Bénard, Henri. Original thermal-convection experiments: two notes in *Comptes rendus de l'Académie des sciences* (1900); PhD thesis defended 15 March 1901, giving the first full description of the hexagonal cellular convection pattern ("Bénard cells"). — VERIFIED: the hexagonal-cell observation is correctly attributed to Bénard's own 1900–1901 work (predating the later Rayleigh–Bénard theoretical formalisation).
- Prigogine, Ilya. Nobel Prize in Chemistry, 1977, "for his contributions to non-equilibrium thermodynamics, particularly the theory of dissipative structures." — VERIFIED against NobelPrize.org. (Prigogine born 25 Jan 1917, died 28 May 2003 — VERIFIED.)
- Belousov, Boris P. Discovery of the oscillating reaction in 1950; repeatedly rejected by journals as "clearly impossible," finally published as a brief abstract in 1959 in an obscure radiation-medicine collection. Anatol Zhabotinsky's elaboration (replacing citric with malonic acid) followed in the 1960s. — VERIFIED; the "long disbelieved" characterisation is accurate. See Kiprijanov, "Chaos and beauty in a beaker: The early history of the Belousov–Zhabotinsky reaction," *Annalen der Physik* (2016).
- *Macrotermes* mound thermoregulation/ventilation. — VERIFIED: fungus-growing Macrotermitinae maintain an internal microclimate (optimal fungal growth ~30 °C, high humidity, low CO₂); ventilation/gas-exchange account associated with J. Scott Turner's work on *Macrotermes michaelseni*. The chapter's "taller than a person," "endure for decades," and precise thermoregulation claims are consistent with the literature.
- Haken, Hermann. *Synergetics: An Introduction*. Springer, 1977. — VERIFIED: first edition 1977; the laser as a nonequilibrium system exhibiting a phase-transition-like onset of coherent (cooperative) emission is Haken's signature example (Haken & Graham, 1970).
- Ashby, W. Ross. "Principles of the Self-Organizing System." In H. von Foerster & G. W. Zopf Jr. (eds.), *Principles of Self-Organization: Transactions of the University of Illinois Symposium*, Pergamon, London, 1962, pp. 255–278. — VERIFIED (title, editors, publisher, pages). Ashby's cybernetic framing of self-organisation dates to the 1940s–1950s (cf. his 1947 "Principles of the Self-Organizing Dynamic System").
