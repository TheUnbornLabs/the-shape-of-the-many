# Citation Policy — binding

Honest sourcing is the single most important rule in this project. A hundred chapters fail exactly here if it slips.

## The binding rules

1. **Never invent** authors, titles, journals, years, DOIs, URLs, page numbers, statistics, or quotations. Not once.
2. **Quotes stay short and attributed** — brief, in quotation marks, with a real source. Paraphrase by default.
3. **Verify or flag.** Where a claim needs support you cannot confirm from reliable knowledge, either web-search to confirm it, or mark it clearly with the flag: **"Further reading to verify before final publication."**
4. **Replication humility.** Where a classic finding is contested or failed to replicate (Milgram's framing, ego-depletion, many priming studies, some Asch interpretations), say so in that chapter's critique beat and note it in references.
5. **WEIRD caution.** Flag where a "universal" claim rests on a narrow Western/student sample.

## references.md structure (per chapter)

Two parts:

```markdown
# References — NN. <Title>

## Confirmed sources
Real, verifiable works underpinning the chapter. Format:
- Author(s). *Title*. Publisher/Journal, Year. — one line on what it supports.

## Further reading to verify before final publication
Anything specific-but-unconfirmed at drafting time. These get resolved in the
Phase 6 citation-verification pass before publication.
- <claim or source> — what needs checking.
```

## Verification cadence

- During drafting: web-verify the core identity facts (dates, founding study, the headline claim) of each chapter; pin real sources.
- Before publication (Phase 6): a dedicated pass web-checks every chapter's headline claims against authoritative sources, corrects and dates them, and clears the "to verify" flags. Keep a **Verification corrections log** of anything materially changed.
