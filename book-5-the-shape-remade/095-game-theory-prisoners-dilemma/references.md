# References — 95. Game theory & the prisoner's dilemma

## Confirmed sources

- von Neumann, John, and Oskar Morgenstern. *Theory of Games and Economic Behavior*. Princeton University Press, 1944. — The founding text of formal game theory; establishes the mathematical study of strategic interaction and zero-sum games.
- Nash, John F. "Equilibrium Points in N-Person Games." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, vol. 36, no. 1 (1950), pp. 48–49. — Introduces and proves existence of the Nash equilibrium, the field's central solution concept.
- Poundstone, William. *Prisoner's Dilemma*. Doubleday, 1992. — Popular history of the dilemma, von Neumann, and the RAND Corporation; source for the Flood–Dresher–Tucker origin story.
- Güth, Werner, Rolf Schmittberger, and Bernd Schwarze. "An Experimental Analysis of Ultimatum Bargaining." *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization*, vol. 3, no. 4 (1982), pp. 367–388. — The original ultimatum game experiment; foundational evidence that people reject unfair offers, contradicting pure self-interest.
- Axelrod, Robert. *The Evolution of Cooperation*. Basic Books, 1984. — Cross-referenced (item 96); the tournament study showing conditional cooperation (Tit for Tat) succeeds in the repeated prisoner's dilemma.
