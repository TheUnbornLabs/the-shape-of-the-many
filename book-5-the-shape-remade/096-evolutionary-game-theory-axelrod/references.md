# References — 96. The evolution of cooperation

## Confirmed sources

- Maynard Smith, J., & Price, G. R. "The Logic of Animal Conflict." *Nature*, 246, 1973, pp. 15–18. — Introduces game theory into evolution and the concept later formalised as the evolutionarily stable strategy (ESS).
- Maynard Smith, J. *Evolution and the Theory of Games*. Cambridge University Press, 1982. — Book-length development and naming of the ESS concept; standard reference for evolutionary game theory.
- Axelrod, R., & Hamilton, W. D. "The Evolution of Cooperation." *Science*, 211 (4489), 1981, pp. 1390–1396. — The tournament analysis and the argument that reciprocity (tit-for-tat) can evolve and remain stable in the iterated prisoner's dilemma. Anatol Rapoport's tit-for-tat, the shortest program submitted, won the first tournament (confirmed).
- Ashworth, Tony. *Trench Warfare 1914–1918: The Live and Let Live System*. Macmillan, 1980. — The historical study, drawn on by Axelrod 1984, documenting the tacit truces (deliberate off-target fire, shouted apologies, high-command countermeasures such as forced raids and unit rotation) on the Western Front.
- Axelrod, R. *The Evolution of Cooperation*. Basic Books, 1984. — The popular exposition; source for the computer tournaments, Rapoport's tit-for-tat, the four properties (nice, retaliatory, forgiving, clear), the "shadow of the future," and the WWI trench "live and let live" case study.
- Nowak, M. A. "Five Rules for the Evolution of Cooperation." *Science*, 314 (5805), 2006, pp. 1560–1563. — Synthesises kin selection, direct reciprocity, indirect reciprocity, network reciprocity, and group selection as distinct mechanisms for cooperation.
- Nowak, M. A., & Sigmund, K. "A strategy of win-stay, lose-shift that outperforms tit-for-tat in the Prisoner's Dilemma game." *Nature*, 364, 1993, pp. 56–58. — Establishes the Pavlov / win-stay, lose-shift strategy and its advantages over tit-for-tat.
- Press, W. H., & Dyson, F. J. "Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma contains strategies that dominate any evolutionary opponent." *PNAS*, 109 (26), 2012, pp. 10409–10413. — Proves the existence of zero-determinant (including extortionate) strategies, complicating the cooperative reading of the iterated game.
- Trivers, R. L. "The Evolution of Reciprocal Altruism." *Quarterly Review of Biology*, 46 (1), 1971, pp. 35–57. — Cross-referenced (item 14); the biological account of reciprocity that tit-for-tat formalises.
- Ostrom, E. *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action*. Cambridge University Press, 1990. — Cross-referenced (items 66, 69); real communities escaping the commons trap via repeated, reputation-bearing interaction.
