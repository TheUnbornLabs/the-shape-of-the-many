# References — 93. Crowdsourcing & citizen science

## Confirmed sources

- Howe, Jeff. "The Rise of Crowdsourcing." *Wired*, June 2006. — The article that coined "crowdsourcing"; anchors the origin beat and the definition of distributing tasks to a large undefined group.
- Surowiecki, James. *The Wisdom of Crowds*. Doubleday, 2004. — The four conditions (diversity, independence, decentralisation, aggregation) that crowdsourcing operationalises; cross-referenced as item 20.
- Lintott, Chris J., et al. "Galaxy Zoo: morphologies derived from visual inspection of galaxies from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey." *Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society*, Vol. 389, Issue 3, 2008, pp. 1179–1189. — The anchor-example paper: method, volunteer numbers, and consensus classification via redundancy.
- Cooper, Seth, et al. "Predicting protein structures with a multiplayer online game." *Nature*, Vol. 466, 12 August 2010, pp. 756–760. — Foldit and the demonstration that gamers can solve structures that stumped algorithms; supports the human-weight beat. (Verified: Nature 466:756–760, DOI 10.1038/nature09304.)
- Khatib, Firas, et al. "Crystal structure of a monomeric retroviral protease solved by protein folding game players." *Nature Structural & Molecular Biology*, Vol. 18, 2011, pp. 1175–1177. — The 2011 retroviral-protease (M-PMV) result solved by Foldit players and credited to the "Foldit Contenders Group"; the specific human-weight-beat example. (Verified: Nat Struct Mol Biol 18:1175–1177, DOI 10.1038/nsmb.2119. NOTE: this result appeared here, NOT in *Nature*.)
- von Ahn, Luis, et al. "reCAPTCHA: Human-Based Character Recognition via Web Security Measures." *Science*, Vol. 321, Issue 5895, 2008, pp. 1465–1468. — Human computation; the digitisation-of-books-via-CAPTCHA mechanism.
- Gray, Mary L., and Siddharth Suri. *Ghost Work: How to Stop Silicon Valley from Building a New Global Underclass*. Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019. — The "ghost work" concept underpinning the beat-5 flip and beat-6 frontier: invisible, precarious micro-labour behind "automated" systems.
