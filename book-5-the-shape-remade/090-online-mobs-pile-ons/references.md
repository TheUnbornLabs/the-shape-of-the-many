# References — 90. Online mobs & pile-ons

## Confirmed sources

- Ronson, Jon. *So You've Been Publicly Shamed*. Riverhead Books / Picador, 2015. — The anchor journalism: vivid case studies of lives dismantled by online pile-ons over minor or misread offences, and the explicit link back to historical public-shaming punishments (stocks, pillory). Source for the structure of the pile-on told without re-naming a specific private target.

- Citron, Danielle Keats. *Hate Crimes in Cyberspace*. Harvard University Press, 2014. — Legal-scholarly documentation of coordinated online harassment: threats, doxxing, defamation, the disproportionate targeting of women, and the failures of law and platforms to respond. Supports the harassment end of the spectrum and the stratification point (who is targeted).

- boyd, danah. *It's Complicated: The Social Lives of Networked Teens*. Yale University Press, 2014. — Source for "context collapse": the flattening of multiple distinct audiences into a single undifferentiated networked public. (boyd styles her name in lowercase.)

- Marwick, Alice E. Work on networked publics, online "drama," and networked shaming — including Marwick & danah boyd, "I tweet honestly, I tweet passionately: Twitter users, context collapse, and the imagined audience," *New Media & Society*, 2011. — Supports the mechanics of how shaming propagates through overlapping networks and the imagined-audience/context-collapse dynamics.
