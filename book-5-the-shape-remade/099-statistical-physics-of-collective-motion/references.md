# References — 99. The statistical physics of collective motion

## Confirmed sources

- Vicsek, T., Czirók, A., Ben-Jacob, E., Cohen, I., & Shochet, O. "Novel Type of Phase Transition in a System of Self-Driven Particles." *Physical Review Letters* 75, 1226–1229, 1995. — The founding model: self-propelled particles with local alignment plus noise, and the order-disorder phase transition as noise falls or density rises.
- Toner, J., & Tu, Y. "Long-Range Order in a Two-Dimensional Dynamical XY Model: How Birds Fly Together." *Physical Review Letters* 75, 4326–4329, 1995. — The hydrodynamic/continuum theory of flocking; explains persistence of order in two dimensions.
- Buhl, J., Sumpter, D. J. T., Couzin, I. D., Hale, J. J., Despland, E., Miller, E. R., & Simpson, S. J. "From Disorder to Order in Marching Locusts." *Science* 312, 1402–1406, 2006. — The ring-arena experiment showing a density-driven transition to collective marching in desert locust nymphs; the chapter's anchor example.
- Ballerini, M., Cabibbo, N., Candelier, R., Cavagna, A., et al. "Interaction ruling animal collective behavior depends on topological rather than metric distance: Evidence from a field study." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 105, 1232–1237, 2008. — STARFLAG reconstruction of starling murmurations; topological interaction and scale-free correlations (cross-referenced as item 3).
- Marchetti, M. C., Joanny, J. F., Ramaswamy, S., Liverpool, T. B., Prost, J., Rao, M., & Simha, R. A. "Hydrodynamics of soft active matter." *Reviews of Modern Physics* 85, 1143–1189, 2013. — Field-defining review of active matter, spanning flocks, bacterial suspensions, and cytoskeletal systems.

- Grégoire, G., & Chaté, H. "Onset of Collective and Cohesive Motion." *Physical Review Letters* 92, 025702, 2004. — Argues that the Vicsek flocking transition is discontinuous (first-order) rather than continuous, including for the minimal Vicsek model; reference confirmed. (Vicsek et al. 1995 originally described the transition as continuous; the debate turns on system size and model specification.)
