# References — 91. Wikipedia & open source as stigmergy

## Confirmed sources

- Raymond, Eric S. *The Cathedral and the Bazaar: Musings on Linux and Open Source by an Accidental Revolutionary*. O'Reilly, 1999 (essay first presented 1997). — Source for the cathedral/bazaar contrast and "Linus's Law" ("given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow"); the many-eyes principle applied to code.
- Benkler, Yochai. *The Wealth of Networks: How Social Production Transforms Markets and Freedom*. Yale University Press, 2006. — Source for "commons-based peer production" and the argument that networked peer production sits outside both market and firm.
- Ostrom, Elinor. *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action*. Cambridge University Press, 1990. — Underpins the governance argument (item 69): durable commons require clear rules, graduated sanctions, and conflict-resolution mechanisms; applied here to the knowledge commons.
- Giles, Jim. "Internet encyclopaedias go head to head." *Nature*, vol. 438, no. 7070, 15 December 2005, pp. 900–901. — The comparison of science entries in Wikipedia and *Encyclopaedia Britannica* by expert reviewers. VERIFIED: 42 article pairs reviewed; reviewers found 162 errors in Wikipedia entries vs 123 in Britannica (averages 3.9 vs 2.9 per article — reported as "about four" vs "about three"), most of them minor. DOI 10.1038/438900a. Britannica published a rebuttal ("Fatally Flawed," March 2006) disputing the methods; *Nature* stood by the study ("Britannica attacks," *Nature* 440:582, 2006).
- Reagle, Joseph M. *Good Faith Collaboration: The Culture of Wikipedia*. MIT Press, 2010. — Source for Wikipedia's governance culture, norms, and the frictions and exclusions that governance produces.
- Torvalds, Linus. Original comp.os.minix announcement of the Linux kernel, 25 August 1991. — Founding event of Linux as a bazaar-style collaborative project.
- Stallman, Richard. GNU Project announcement, 1983; the free-software movement. — Origin of the free/open-source software lineage.
