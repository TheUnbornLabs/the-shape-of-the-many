# References — 58. The first-follower dynamic

## Confirmed sources

- Sivers, Derek. "How to Start a Movement." TED (TED2010), February 2010. — The popular framing of the first-follower dynamic; the dancing-man festival video and the "first follower transforms a lone nut into a leader" formulation. Treated in-chapter as popular framing, not evidence.
- Granovetter, Mark. "Threshold Models of Collective Behavior." *American Journal of Sociology* 83, no. 6 (1978): 1420–1443. — The rigorous mechanism: individual thresholds, the instigator at threshold zero, the first follower at threshold one, and the cascade condition (dense vs. gapped threshold distributions). The mathematical anchor for the chapter and for item 7.
- Centola, Damon. *How Behavior Spreads: The Science of Complex Contagions*. Princeton University Press, 2018. — The complex-contagion refinement: costly/risky behaviours require multiple reinforcing exposures, so a single follower is often insufficient and a small early cluster is needed. Anchors item 54.
- Asch, Solomon E. "Opinions and Social Pressure." *Scientific American* 193, no. 5 (1955): 31–35. — The single-ally finding: one dissenting partner sharply reduces conformity to a unanimous false majority. The laboratory proof cited for the first follower as ally (item 35).
- Kuran, Timur. *Private Truths, Public Lies: The Social Consequences of Preference Falsification*. Harvard University Press, 1995. — The preference-falsification / pluralistic-ignorance mechanism that the first-follower dynamic mirrors and reverses; the reservoir of hidden dissent, threshold cascades, and unanticipated collapse. Anchors the flip and human-weight beats and items 34, 42, 85.
