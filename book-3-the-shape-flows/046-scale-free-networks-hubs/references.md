# References — 46. Scale-free networks & hubs

## Confirmed sources

- Barabási, Albert-László, and Réka Albert. "Emergence of scaling in random networks." *Science* 286, no. 5439 (1999): 509–512. — The founding paper of the field: introduces the scale-free observation, the BA model, and the growth-plus-preferential-attachment mechanism generating a power-law degree distribution P(k) ~ k^(-3).
- Price, Derek J. de Solla. "Networks of scientific papers." *Science* 149, no. 3683 (1965): 510–515. — Early empirical demonstration of a skewed (power-law-like) citation distribution and the "cumulative advantage" mechanism, a precursor of preferential attachment.
- Clauset, Aaron, Cosma Rohilla Shalizi, and M. E. J. Newman. "Power-law distributions in empirical data." *SIAM Review* 51, no. 4 (2009): 661–703. — The standard statistical methodology for fitting and testing power laws; shows how error-prone naive (log-log line-fitting) exponent estimation is.
- Broido, Anna D., and Aaron Clauset. "Scale-free networks are rare." *Nature Communications* 10 (2019): article 1017. — Tests ~1,000 real networks and argues that strict scale-free structure is statistically uncommon; many networks are better fit by other heavy-tailed distributions (e.g. log-normal).
- Simon, Herbert A. "On a class of skew distribution functions." *Biometrika* 42, no. 3/4 (1955): 425–440. — Rigorous general treatment of "rich-get-richer" processes generating skewed distributions; the mathematical ancestor of preferential-attachment models.
