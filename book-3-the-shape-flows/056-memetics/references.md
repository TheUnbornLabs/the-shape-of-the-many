# References — 56. Memetics

## Confirmed sources

- Dawkins, Richard. *The Selfish Gene*. Oxford University Press, 1976. — The origin: coins "meme" in the final chapter as a cultural replicator spreading "from brain to brain"; supplies the gene's-eye view and the fidelity/fecundity/longevity criteria for replicator success.
- Dennett, Daniel C. *Consciousness Explained*. Little, Brown, 1991. — Develops memes as central to a theory of mind and self; argues Darwinian selection among ideas does real explanatory work.
- Dennett, Daniel C. *Darwin's Dangerous Idea: Evolution and the Meanings of Life*. Simon & Schuster, 1995. — Treats the Darwinian algorithm as substrate-neutral; extends memetic thinking to culture.
- Blackmore, Susan. *The Meme Machine*. Oxford University Press, 1999. — The strongest statement of the meme's-eye view: imitation as the human breakthrough; memes spreading for their own sake; the self as a memeplex.
- Sperber, Dan. *Explaining Culture: A Naturalistic Approach*. Blackwell, 1996. — The central critique: cultural transmission is reconstructive and transformative, not replicative; the "cultural attraction" alternative to high-fidelity copying.
- Aunger, Robert (ed.). *Darwinizing Culture: The Status of Memetics as a Science*. Oxford University Press, 2000. — Edited volume staging the proponent–sceptic debate; documents the framework's contested scientific status.
