# References — 44. Small-world networks

## Confirmed sources

- Milgram, Stanley. "The Small World Problem." *Psychology Today*, vol. 1, no. 1, 1967, pp. 60–67. — The original popular account of the acquaintance-chain experiment and the "small world" framing.
- Travers, Jeffrey, and Stanley Milgram. "An Experimental Study of the Small World Problem." *Sociometry*, vol. 32, no. 4, 1969, pp. 425–443. — The formal write-up; source for the routing method and the median completed-chain length of roughly five to six intermediaries.
- Watts, Duncan J., and Steven H. Strogatz. "Collective dynamics of 'small-world' networks." *Nature*, vol. 393, 1998, pp. 440–442. — The foundational model: the ring-lattice rewiring construction showing that a few random shortcuts collapse average path length while preserving high clustering.
- Kleinberg, Jon M. "Navigation in a small world." *Nature*, vol. 406, 2000, p. 845. — Proves that short paths existing does not mean they are findable by decentralised (local, greedy) search, except under a special distribution of long-range links. Underpins the searchability critique.
- Kleinfeld, Judith S. "The small world problem." *Society*, vol. 39, no. 2, 2002, pp. 61–66. — The principal critical reassessment of Milgram: low completion rates, possible sample bias, and the point that "six degrees" derives only from completed chains.
