# References — 45. The strength of weak ties

## Confirmed sources

- Granovetter, Mark S. "The Strength of Weak Ties." *American Journal of Sociology*, vol. 78, no. 6, 1973, pp. 1360–1380. — The founding paper; the forbidden-triad argument, the bridge-must-be-weak result, and the Newton, Massachusetts job-search interviews. One of the most-cited papers in social science.
- Granovetter, Mark S. "The Strength of Weak Ties: A Network Theory Revisited." *Sociological Theory*, vol. 1, 1983, pp. 201–233. — Granovetter's own decade-later defence and extension of the theory, addressing critics and applying it to poverty, community organisation, and diffusion. Supports the beat-6 material on mobility and exclusion.
- Rajkumar, Karthik; Saint-Jacques, Guillaume; Bojinov, Iavor; Brynjolfsson, Erik; Aral, Sinan. "A causal test of the strength of weak ties." *Science*, vol. 377, no. 6612, 2022, pp. 1304–1310. — Large-scale LinkedIn "People You May Know" experiments; causal vindication of the theory, with the non-monotonic ("moderately weak ties best") refinement. Supports beat 7 and the closing image.
