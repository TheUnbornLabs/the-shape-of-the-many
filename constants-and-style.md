# Constants & Style — The Shape of the Many

**Read this file at the start of every batch.** It is the single source for the thesis, voice, motif, and cross-reference map. Consistency across all five books is the product.

---

## The thesis (one line, restated in every book)

**The wise crowd and the mob are the same people.** One mechanism — local interaction producing group-level order — runs under different conditions, and a nameable condition flips the sign between collective wisdom and collective catastrophe. The conditions that flip it are chiefly: **independence, diversity, and whether people act before or after they see each other.**

Every book restates this in its own register. The reader should be unable to forget it. Never present a "good crowd" and a "bad crowd" as different species — they are one crowd under two conditions. Say so, repeatedly, in different words.

## The signature frontier (keep visible, never forced)

This apparatus points at how **orders of imposed silence sustain themselves and how they break** — caste, hierarchy, and social stratification as self-reproducing collective-behaviour systems held in place by hidden dissent (preference falsification, item 34), and broken when the silence breaks. Surface it wherever an item genuinely illuminates it; leave it alone where it doesn't. Never bury it, never bolt it on.

## The motif

The word **Shape** recurs deliberately as the connective image: form rising, breaking, flowing, holding, being remade. The five books are movements of one image:

- Book One — **The Shape Forms** (I–III: emergence, evolution, the wise crowd)
- Book Two — **The Shape Breaks** (IV–V: cascades, panics, conformity)
- Book Three — **The Shape Flows** (VI–VII: networks, contagion)
- Book Four — **The Shape Holds Power** (VIII–X: markets, governance, ideology)
- Book Five — **The Shape Remade** (XI–XII: digital swarms, the toolkit)

## Voice (locked across all five books)

- Essayistic and precise; literary without losing rigour. A serious trade non-fiction book — **not a textbook, not a listicle.**
- **Prose, not bullet-spam.** Argue in paragraphs inside chapters. Reserve lists for genuine enumeration.
- Examples are **memory hooks**: concrete, specific, occasionally surprising. One anchor example per chapter that the reader will remember.
- Read the poles in pairs. Every wise-crowd item has a mob twin; name it.
- **No filler.** No "In today's world…", no "It is important to note…", no hollow summarising, no AI throat-clearing. Cut anything a discerning editor would cut.
- Restrained lyricism at movement openings and closings; discipline in the body.
- Avoid "genuinely", "honestly", "straightforward".

## Per-chapter contract (identical for all 100 items)

Two files per item folder `NNN-slug/`:
- **`chapter.md`** — the full chapter following the 7-beat template (see `writing-template.md`).
- **`references.md`** — real sources only (see `citation-policy.md`).

The seven beats (present in every chapter, length breathing with the concept):
1. **The idea** — clear, full statement in plain language.
2. **The origin** — thinker(s)/study/event, with real dates and sources.
3. **The mechanism** — the causal gears, not just the label.
4. **The anchor example** — one vivid case, the reader's memory hook.
5. **The flip** — how it connects to the thesis; the condition that tips it; its opposite pole (cross-reference the paired item).
6. **The human weight** — real stakes (leadership, oppression, markets, belief); frontier surfaced where it applies.
7. **The critique** — limits, replication failures, contested status. Honesty, not reverence.

Chapter length target: ~1,500–3,000 words, breathing with depth. (Books reach ~200 pages via chapters + movement intros + cluster bridges + paired-pole callouts + closing synthesis — never padding.)

## Book front/back matter (written last, per book)

- **Movement introduction** — an essay stating the thesis in this book's register, previewing the arc.
- **Cluster bridge essays** — short connective pieces between clusters.
- **Paired-pole callouts** — brief cross-reference boxes linking each item to its twin.
- **Closing synthesis** — pulls the movement together, hands off to the next book by name.
