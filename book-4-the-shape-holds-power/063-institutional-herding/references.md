# References — 63. Institutional herding

## Confirmed sources

- Keynes, John Maynard. *The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money*. Macmillan, 1936. — Chapter 12, on long-term expectation; the source of "it is better for reputation to fail conventionally than to succeed unconventionally" and the foundational observation that professional investors are rewarded for conformity over correctness.
- Scharfstein, David S., and Jeremy C. Stein. "Herd Behavior and Investment." *American Economic Review*, vol. 80, no. 3 (June 1990), pp. 465–479. — The formal model of reputational herding; managers mimic to "share the blame" and protect their standing in the managerial labour market.
- Lakonishok, Josef, Andrei Shleifer, and Robert W. Vishny. "The Impact of Institutional Trading on Stock Prices." *Journal of Financial Economics*, vol. 32, no. 1 (1992), pp. 23–43. — Introduces the widely used LSV measure of institutional herding (whether institutions trade a stock in the same direction more than chance predicts). Supports the critique beat on contested measurement.
- Devenow, Anat, and Ivo Welch. "Rational Herding in Financial Economics." *European Economic Review*, vol. 40, nos. 3–5 (April 1996), pp. 603–615. — Survey distinguishing informational, reputational, and compensation-driven (rational) herding; supports the mechanism taxonomy.
- Bikhchandani, Sushil, and Sunil Sharma. "Herd Behavior in Financial Markets." *IMF Staff Papers*, vol. 47, no. 3 (2000), pp. 279–310. — Survey extending the herding taxonomy toward financial-market and policy questions.
