# References — 66. The tragedy of the commons — and Ostrom's rebuttal

## Confirmed sources

- Hardin, Garrett. "The Tragedy of the Commons." *Science* 162, no. 3859 (1968): 1243–1248. — The founding essay; the pasture image, the "freedom in a commons brings ruin to all" argument, and the "mutual coercion, mutually agreed upon" prescription of privatisation or state control.
- Ostrom, Elinor. *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action*. Cambridge University Press, 1990. — The empirical rebuttal; the case studies (including Törbel in the Swiss Valais, Japanese village commons, Spanish irrigation *huertas*, Philippine fisheries) and the design principles for durable self-governance; source for the Törbel *Winterung* rule tying summer alp grazing to winter hay capacity, the 1517 village statute, and bounded membership with elected monitoring and fines.
- Ostrom, Elinor; Burger, Joanna; Field, Christopher B.; Norgaard, Richard B.; Policansky, David. "Revisiting the Commons: Local Lessons, Global Challenges." *Science* 284, no. 5412 (1999): 278–282. — On the limits of local commons solutions, the problem of scale, mobile resources, and how self-governance can fail.
- The Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel 2009, awarded to Elinor Ostrom (shared with Oliver E. Williamson) "for her analysis of economic governance, especially the commons." Ostrom was the first woman awarded the economics prize. — Nobel citation (verified against the official Nobel record, nobelprize.org).
- Southern Poverty Law Center, "Garrett Hardin," Extremist Files (splcenter.org). — Confirms Hardin's documented nativism and eugenics: SPLC describes his publications as "frank in their racism and quasi-fascist ethnonationalism"; he served on the boards of the anti-immigrant Federation for American Immigration Reform and the white-nationalist Social Contract Press, co-founded Californians for Population Stabilization and The Environmental Fund, was a member of the American Eugenics Society (from 1956; director 1971–1974), and received Pioneer Fund support. Supports the human-weight beat's characterisation of Hardin's politics.
- Netting, Robert McC. *Balancing on an Alp: Ecological Change and Continuity in a Swiss Mountain Community*. Cambridge University Press, 1981. — The ethnographic study of Törbel on which Ostrom draws for the *Winterung* rule, the 1517 statute, bounded membership, and elected monitoring.
