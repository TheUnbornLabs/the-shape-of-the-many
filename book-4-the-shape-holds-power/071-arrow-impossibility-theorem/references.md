# References — 71. Arrow's impossibility theorem

## Confirmed sources

- Arrow, Kenneth J. *Social Choice and Individual Values*. Cowles Commission Monograph No. 12, John Wiley & Sons, 1951 (2nd ed. 1963). — The founding statement of the impossibility theorem and its four conditions (unrestricted domain, Pareto, independence of irrelevant alternatives, non-dictatorship).
- Condorcet, Marie Jean Antoine Nicolas de Caritat, Marquis de. *Essai sur l'application de l'analyse à la probabilité des décisions rendues à la pluralité des voix.* Imprimerie Royale, Paris, 1785. — Origin of the voting paradox (the intransitive majority cycle) that seeds Arrow's result.
- Black, Duncan. "On the Rationale of Group Decision-Making." *Journal of Political Economy*, vol. 56, no. 1, 1948, pp. 23–34. — Introduces single-peaked preferences and the median voter result, the principal domain-restriction escape from the Condorcet cycle.
- Gibbard, Allan. "Manipulation of Voting Schemes: A General Result." *Econometrica*, vol. 41, no. 4, 1973, pp. 587–601. — Manipulability (strategic voting) as an impossibility for non-dictatorial rules over 3+ options.
- Satterthwaite, Mark A. "Strategy-proofness and Arrow's Conditions." *Journal of Economic Theory*, vol. 10, no. 2, 1975, pp. 187–217. — Independent proof of the manipulation result, jointly the Gibbard–Satterthwaite theorem.
- Sen, Amartya. "The Impossibility of a Paretian Liberal." *Journal of Political Economy*, vol. 78, no. 1, 1970, pp. 152–157. — The liberal paradox: Pareto unanimity and minimal individual liberty cannot both be honoured.
- Sen, Amartya. *Collective Choice and Social Welfare.* Holden-Day, 1970. — Standard treatment situating Arrow's theorem within social choice theory.
- Arrow's Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences (1972, shared with John Hicks), awarded at age 51 — web-confirmed that Arrow remains the youngest recipient of the economics prize (RAND, Stanford, and standard biographical records concur). Cited for the chapter's "youngest laureate" claim in "The origin."
