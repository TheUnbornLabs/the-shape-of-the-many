# References — 62. Minsky's financial instability hypothesis

## Confirmed sources

- Minsky, Hyman P. *Stabilizing an Unstable Economy*. Yale University Press, 1986. — The book-length statement of the hypothesis; supplies the central thesis that an economy which requires stabilising does not stabilise itself, and the account of financing regimes.
- Minsky, Hyman P. "The Financial Instability Hypothesis." Levy Economics Institute of Bard College, Working Paper No. 74, 1992. — The compact late statement; source for the two theorems (stable and unstable financing regimes; drift from stable toward unstable over prolonged prosperity) and the hedge/speculative/Ponzi taxonomy.
- Minsky, Hyman P. *John Maynard Keynes*. Columbia University Press, 1975. — Minsky's reading of Keynes on uncertainty and financial fragility; supports the account of his intellectual roots.
- Kindleberger, Charles P. *Manias, Panics, and Crashes: A History of Financial Crises*. Basic Books, 1978. — The historical companion to Minsky's theory; documents the recurrent boom-to-bust pattern across centuries and popularised a Minsky-influenced narrative of crises among economic historians.
- McCulley, Paul. Coined the term "Minsky moment" in 1998, writing at PIMCO in the context of the Russian financial crisis; the phrase entered wide circulation during and after the 2008 crisis. — Source for the origin and later popularisation of the term.
