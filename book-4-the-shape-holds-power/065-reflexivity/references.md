# References — 65. Reflexivity

## Confirmed sources

- Soros, George. *The Alchemy of Finance: Reading the Mind of the Market*. Simon & Schuster, 1987. — The founding statement of reflexivity as an operating theory of markets; the cognitive/participating distinction and the credit boom-bust illustration.
- Soros, George. "Fallibility, reflexivity, and the human uncertainty principle." *Journal of Economic Methodology*, vol. 20, no. 4, 2013, pp. 309–329. — Soros's later, more formal restatement of the theory, including the human uncertainty principle and his acknowledgement of the framework's limits.
- Merton, Robert K. "The Self-Fulfilling Prophecy." *The Antioch Review*, vol. 8, no. 2, 1948, pp. 193–210. — The full mechanism: a false definition of a situation evoking behaviour that makes the false definition true.
- Thomas, W. I., and Dorothy Swaine Thomas. *The Child in America: Behavior Problems and Programs*. Alfred A. Knopf, 1928. — Source of the Thomas theorem: "If men define situations as real, they are real in their consequences" (p. 572).
- Popper, Karl R. *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (English ed., Hutchinson, 1959; German orig. *Logik der Forschung*, 1934) and *The Open Society and Its Enemies* (Routledge, 1945). — The fallibilist philosophy of science Soros credits as the root of reflexivity; the falsifiability criterion invoked in the critique.
