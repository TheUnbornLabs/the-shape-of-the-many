# References — 73. Liquid & delegated democracy

## Confirmed sources

- Ford, Bryan. *Delegative Democracy*. Online essay/manuscript, 2002. — The most-cited modern articulation of the concept; lays out free choice between direct voting and delegation, transitivity, and per-issue specialisation. Circulated online rather than in a peer-reviewed journal; underpins the "idea" and "mechanism" beats.
- Tullock, Gordon. *Toward a Mathematics of Politics*. University of Michigan Press, 1967. — Early sketch of a scheme allowing citizens to vote directly or assign votes to chosen representatives, revisable at will; cited as an intellectual ancestor of delegative democracy.
- Blum, Christian, and Christina Isabel Zuber. "Liquid Democracy: Potentials, Problems, and Perspectives." *Journal of Political Philosophy*, vol. 24, no. 2, 2016, pp. 162–182. — Scholarly treatment of the normative case for and against liquid democracy; supports the framing of promise versus concentration and accountability problems.
- Kling, Christoph Carl, Jérôme Kunegis, Heinrich Hartmann, Markus Strohmaier, and Steffen Staab. "Voting Behaviour and Power in Online Democracy: A Study of LiquidFeedback in Germany's Pirate Party." *Proceedings of the Ninth International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*, 2015. — Empirical analysis of the LiquidFeedback delegation data documenting super-voter concentration; underpins the anchor example and critique.
- Behrens, Jan, Axel Kistner, Andreas Nitsche, and Björn Swierczek. *The Principles of LiquidFeedback*. Interaktive Demokratie e.V., 2014. — Account by the platform's developers of LiquidFeedback's design goals and mechanics, candid about ambitions and strains; supports the mechanism and anchor beats.
