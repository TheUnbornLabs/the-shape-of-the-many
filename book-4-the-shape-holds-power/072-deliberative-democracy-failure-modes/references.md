# References — 72. Deliberative democracy and its failure modes

## Confirmed sources

- Jürgen Habermas. *The Theory of Communicative Action*, Vol. 1: *Reason and the Rationalization of Society*. Beacon Press, 1984 (German original *Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns*, 1981). — Source for the distinction between strategic and communicative action and the regulative ideal of the "ideal speech situation."
- James S. Fishkin. *When the People Speak: Deliberative Democracy and Public Consultation*. Oxford University Press, 2009. — The definitive statement of the Deliberative Poll: representative sampling, balanced briefing, moderated small-group discussion, and pre/post surveys measuring opinion change.
- Cass R. Sunstein. "The Law of Group Polarization." *Journal of Political Philosophy*, Vol. 10, No. 2, 2002, pp. 175–195. — Statement of group polarization: like-minded deliberators move toward a more extreme point in the direction they already leaned; the persuasive-arguments and social-comparison mechanisms.
- Hélène Landemore. *Open Democracy: Reinventing Popular Rule for the Twenty-First Century*. Princeton University Press, 2020. — The case for sortition and randomly selected deliberating citizens as a central democratic institution.
- Joshua Cohen. "Deliberation and Democratic Legitimacy." In Alan Hamlin and Philip Pettit (eds.), *The Good Polity*. Blackwell, 1989. — Foundational articulation of deliberative legitimacy: law as issuing from deliberation among citizens regarded as equals.
- The Citizens' Assembly (Ireland), convened November 2016; chaired by Ms Justice Mary Laffoy. — Web-confirmed: 99 members (plus the chair) selected to be broadly representative by age, gender, region, and social class; its work on the Eighth Amendment fed into the Oireachtas committee and the 25 May 2018 referendum, which approved repeal (Thirty-sixth Amendment) with 66.4% in favour. Official reports available at citizensassembly.ie.
