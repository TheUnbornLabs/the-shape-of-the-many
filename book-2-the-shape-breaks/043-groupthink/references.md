# References — 43. Groupthink

## Confirmed sources

- Janis, Irving L. *Victims of Groupthink: A Psychological Study of Foreign-Policy Decisions and Fiascoes*. Houghton Mifflin, 1972. — The founding statement of the concept; the comparative case studies (Bay of Pigs, Pearl Harbor, Korea, Vietnam) and the original derivation of the symptoms.
- Janis, Irving L. *Groupthink: Psychological Studies of Policy Decisions and Fiascoes*. 2nd ed. Houghton Mifflin, 1982. — The revised and expanded edition; source for the eight symptoms in three families, the antecedent conditions, and the Cuban Missile Crisis as the counter-case of a re-engineered process.
- 't Hart, Paul. *Groupthink in Government: A Study of Small Groups and Policy Failure*. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1994. — Serious political-science application and critical development of the framework; loosens some of Janis's original causal claims.
- Baron, Robert S. "So Right It's Wrong: Groupthink and the Ubiquitous Nature of Polarized Group Decision Making." *Advances in Experimental Social Psychology*, vol. 37, 2005, pp. 219–253. — Argues the strong causal model is unsupported by experimental evidence while a looser version of concurrence-seeking survives; source for the "ubiquitous but unproven" verdict.
- Esser, James K. "Alive and Well after 25 Years: A Review of Groupthink Research." *Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes*, vol. 73, nos. 2–3, 1998, pp. 116–141. — Review of the experimental literature finding weak and inconsistent support for the full model, particularly for the role of cohesion.
