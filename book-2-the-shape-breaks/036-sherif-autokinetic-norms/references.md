# References — 36. Sherif's autokinetic norms

## Confirmed sources

- Sherif, Muzafer. *A Study of Some Social Factors in Perception*. Archives of Psychology, No. 187, 1935. — The original monograph reporting the autokinetic experiments: individual estimates converging on a group norm under ambiguity.
- Sherif, Muzafer. *The Psychology of Social Norms*. Harper & Brothers, 1936. — The landmark book framing the autokinetic findings as an experimental model for the emergence of social norms; source for the internalisation and norm-persistence claims.
- Jacobs, Robert C., and Donald T. Campbell. "The perpetuation of an arbitrary tradition through several generations of a laboratory microculture." *Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology*, vol. 62, no. 3, 1961, pp. 649–658. — The generational-transmission ("microculture") study: an arbitrary seeded norm passing down a chain of replaced members, and its gradual decay across generations.
- Deutsch, Morton, and Harold B. Gerard. "A study of normative and informational social influences upon individual judgment." *Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology*, vol. 51, no. 3, 1955, pp. 629–636. — Source for the formal distinction between informational and normative influence used to pair Sherif (informational, ambiguous task) against Asch (normative, unambiguous task); developed fully in item 41.
