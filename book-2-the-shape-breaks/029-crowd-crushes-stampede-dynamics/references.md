# References — 29. Crowd crushes & stampede dynamics

## Confirmed sources

- Helbing, D., Farkas, I., & Vicsek, T. *Simulating dynamical features of escape panic*. Nature, 407:487–490, 2000. — The social-force model of pedestrian dynamics; source of the "faster-is-slower" effect at bottlenecks and the physical basis for crowd-flow modelling.
- Helbing, D., & Mukerji, P. *Crowd disasters as systemic failures: analysis of the Love Parade disaster*. EPJ Data Science, 1:7, 2012. — Argues crowd disasters are systemic (density/management) failures rather than failures of individual behaviour; case analysis of the 2010 Duisburg Love Parade crush.
- Helbing, D., Johansson, A., & Al-Abideen, H. Z. *Dynamics of crowd disasters: An empirical study*. Physical Review E, 75:046109, 2007. — Video analysis of the 2006 Mina/Hajj crush; identification of "crowd turbulence" as a distinct high-density regime with involuntary displacements. — Supports the crowd-turbulence claim (verify exact title/citation below).
- Fruin, J. J. *The Causes and Prevention of Crowd Disasters*. In *Engineering for Crowd Safety* (Smith & Dickie, eds.), Elsevier, 1993. — Foundational statement that crowd disasters are engineering/density failures; introduces "dangerous" and "fluid" crowd densities.
- Still, G. K. *Introduction to Crowd Science*. CRC Press, 2014. — Applied crowd-safety science grounded in density, flow, and space geometry; used in mass-gathering safety design.
- Hillsborough Independent Panel. *Report of the Hillsborough Independent Panel*. HC 581, The Stationery Office, 2012. — Official re-examination of the 1989 Hillsborough disaster; established that supporter behaviour was not the cause.
- Hillsborough Inquests (new inquests). Determinations of the jury, 26 April 2016. — Conclusion of "unlawful killing"; finding that the conduct of the fans did not cause or contribute to the disaster.
