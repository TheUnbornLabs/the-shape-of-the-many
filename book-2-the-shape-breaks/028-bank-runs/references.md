# References — 28. Bank runs

## Confirmed sources

- Diamond, Douglas W., and Philip H. Dybvig. "Bank Runs, Deposit Insurance, and Liquidity." *Journal of Political Economy* 91, no. 3 (1983): 401–419. — The canonical model of the chapter: maturity transformation, sequential service, two self-fulfilling equilibria, and the stabilising role of deposit insurance.
- Bryant, John. "A Model of Reserves, Bank Runs, and Deposit Insurance." *Journal of Banking & Finance* 4, no. 4 (1980): 335–344. — Earlier formal treatment showing how deposit insurance can stabilise a fragile banking arrangement; predecessor to Diamond–Dybvig.
- Kuran, Timur. "Sparks and Prairie Fires: A Theory of Unanticipated Political Revolution." *Public Choice* 61, no. 1 (1989): 41–74. — Revolutions as sudden, run-like shifts between equilibria, unpredictable because driven by hidden dissent.
- Kuran, Timur. "Now Out of Never: The Element of Surprise in the East European Revolution of 1989." *World Politics* 44, no. 1 (1991): 7–48. — Applies the preference-falsification / cascade account to a concrete case; underpins the bank-run–revolution analogy in beat 6.
- Kuran, Timur. *Private Truths, Public Lies: The Social Consequences of Preference Falsification.* Harvard University Press, 1995. — Full statement of preference falsification and the sudden collapse of silent orders (cross-referenced to item 34).
- The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences. 2022 Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel, awarded to Ben Bernanke, Douglas W. Diamond, and Philip H. Dybvig for research on banks and financial crises. — Establishes the standing and influence of the Diamond–Dybvig model.
