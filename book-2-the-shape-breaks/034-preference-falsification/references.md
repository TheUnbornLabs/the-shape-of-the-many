# References — 34. Preference falsification

## Confirmed sources

- Kuran, Timur. "Chameleon voters and public choice." *Public Choice* 53(1), 1987, pp. 53–78. — The article in which Kuran coined the term "preference falsification." (Volume and pages web-verified.)
- Kuran, Timur. "Sparks and prairie fires: A theory of unanticipated political revolution." *Public Choice* 61 (1989): 41–74. — The formal theory of how hidden dissent produces sudden, unforeseeable revolutionary cascades; source of the "sparks and prairie fires" image.
- Kuran, Timur. "Now Out of Never: The Element of Surprise in the East European Revolution of 1989." *World Politics* 44 (1991): 7–48. — Applies the theory to the 1989 collapse; the paradigm case of an order whose fragility was hidden by preference falsification.
- Kuran, Timur. *Private Truths, Public Lies: The Social Consequences of Preference Falsification.* Harvard University Press, 1995. — The full synthesis: the three consequences (stability, distortion of public knowledge, concealment of political possibilities), the threshold/cost mechanism, and the historical cases (1789, 1917, 1979, 1989).
- Havel, Václav. "The Power of the Powerless." 1978. — The dissident essay describing "living within the lie" and the greengrocer who displays the regime's slogan; the moral portrait of the condition Kuran later formalised.
- Bicchieri, Cristina. *The Grammar of Society: The Nature and Dynamics of Social Norms.* Cambridge University Press, 2006. — On the structure of social norms and conditional conformity; supports the account of how perceived compliance sustains a norm.
