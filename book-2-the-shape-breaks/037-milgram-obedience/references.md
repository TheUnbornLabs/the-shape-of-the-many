# References — 37. Milgram's obedience studies

## Confirmed sources

- Milgram, Stanley. "Behavioral Study of Obedience." *Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology*, 67(4), 1963, pp. 371–378. — The original published report of the baseline experiment; source for the ~65% obedience-to-450V finding in the voice-feedback condition, the newspaper-recruited New Haven sample, and the deceptive memory-and-learning cover story.
- Milgram, Stanley. *Obedience to Authority: An Experimental View*. Harper & Row, 1974. — The full programme of twenty-four variations; source for the "agentic state" theory, the four scripted prods, the shock-generator design and voltage labels, and the proximity/remoteness and disobedient-peer conditions.
- Arendt, Hannah. *Eichmann in Jerusalem: A Report on the Banality of Evil*. Viking Press, 1963. — Source for the "banality of evil" and the Eichmann-trial context contemporaneous with Milgram's design.
- Perry, Gina. *Behind the Shock Machine: The Untold Story of the Notorious Milgram Psychology Experiments*. Scribe (Australia), 2012; New Press (US), 2013. — Archival re-examination; source for the critique regarding experimenter improvisation beyond the four prods, subjects who suspected the shocks were fake, variation across conditions, and ethical shortcomings.
- Burger, Jerry M. "Replicating Milgram: Would People Still Obey Today?" *American Psychologist*, 64(1), 2009, pp. 1–11. — Ethically constrained partial replication stopping at 150 volts; found obedience rates broadly comparable to Milgram's.
- Haslam, S. Alexander, and Stephen D. Reicher, and collaborators. Work on "engaged followership" reinterpreting the Milgram findings (e.g. Reicher, Haslam & Smith, "Working Toward the Experimenter," *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, 2012; Haslam & Reicher, *PLoS Biology* and related papers). — Source for the identification-based alternative to the agentic-state account.
