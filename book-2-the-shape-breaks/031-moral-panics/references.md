# References — 31. Moral panics

## Confirmed sources

- Cohen, Stanley. *Folk Devils and Moral Panics: The Creation of the Mods and Rockers*. MacGibbon and Kee, 1972. — The founding text; coins "moral panic," anatomises the pattern (threat definition, media stylisation, moral barricades, expert pronouncement, fade), and studies the 1960s British Mods and Rockers seaside clashes. Later editions carry Cohen's reflections on the term's overuse.
- Young, Jock. *The Drugtakers: The Social Meaning of Drug Use*. MacGibbon and Kee / Paladin, 1971. — Develops deviancy amplification and the amplification spiral; contemporary influence on Cohen.
- Hall, Stuart; Critcher, Chas; Jefferson, Tony; Clarke, John; and Roberts, Brian. *Policing the Crisis: Mugging, the State, and Law and Order*. Macmillan, 1978. — Reads the early-1970s British "mugging" panic as an ideological displacement of a wider social crisis onto a racialised folk devil, licensing authoritarian policing.
- Goode, Erich, and Ben-Yehuda, Nachman. *Moral Panics: The Social Construction of Deviance*. Blackwell, 1994. — Supplies the five defining criteria: concern, hostility, consensus, disproportionality, volatility.
- Becker, Howard S. *Outsiders: Studies in the Sociology of Deviance*. Free Press, 1963. — Source of "moral entrepreneurs" and the labelling framework Cohen drew on.
- Satanic ritual abuse panic / McMartin Preschool case (Manhattan Beach, California). Web-verified: the case began with a mother's 1983 allegation; investigators used highly suggestive/leading interview techniques producing fantastical allegations (tunnels, satanic rites); the McMartin proceedings became the longest and most expensive series of criminal trials in American history to that point (roughly seven years, ~$15 million) and ended in 1990 with no convictions. (Sources: Wikipedia "McMartin preschool trial"; Douglas Linder, Famous Trials.)
- Kenneth V. Lanning, FBI Behavioral Science Unit, *Investigator's Guide to Allegations of "Ritual" Child Abuse* (1992). Web-verified as the FBI report concluding that no substantiated evidence of organised satanic-ritual-abuse networks had been found despite thousands of allegations.
- 1964 Mods and Rockers locations and dates. Web-verified: Clacton-on-Sea, Essex, over the Easter weekend of 1964 (~1,000 youths, minor violence exaggerated by the press); Brighton and other seaside resorts over subsequent bank holidays. Consistent with Cohen's account.
