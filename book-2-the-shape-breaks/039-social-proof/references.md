# References — 39. Social proof

## Confirmed sources

- Cialdini, Robert B. *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion*. Harper & Row, 1984. — Popularised the term "social proof" as one of the six principles of influence; source for the compliance framing and the manufactured-proof reading in beats 1, 5, and 6.
- Festinger, Leon. "A Theory of Social Comparison Processes." *Human Relations*, 7(2), 1954, pp. 117–140. — The drive to evaluate one's opinions and abilities against others when no objective standard is available; the deepest root of the mechanism (beat 2).
- Deutsch, Morton, and Harold B. Gerard. "A Study of Normative and Informational Social Influences upon Individual Judgment." *Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology*, 51(3), 1955, pp. 629–636. — The normative/informational distinction that identifies social proof as the informational half of conformity (beats 2 and 5).
- Milgram, Stanley, Leonard Bickman, and Lawrence Berkowitz. "Note on the Drawing Power of Crowds of Different Size." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 13(1), 1969, pp. 79–82. — The sky-gazing field study; crowd size 1–15 confederates, ~40% of passersby looking up for one gazer rising to ~85% for fifteen. Anchor example (beat 4).
- Cialdini, Robert B., Linda J. Demaine, Brad J. Sagarin, Daniel W. Barrett, Kelton Rhoads, and Patricia L. Winter. "Managing Social Norms for Persuasive Impact." *Social Influence*, 1(1), 2006, pp. 3–15. — The Petrified Forest National Park experiment; descriptive-norm messages about how much theft occurs increased theft, while injunctive-norm messages suppressed it. Boomerang effect (beat 7).
- Schultz, P. Wesley, Jessica M. Nolan, Robert B. Cialdini, Noah J. Goldstein, and Vladas Griskevicius. "The Constructive, Destructive, and Reconstructive Power of Social Norms." *Psychological Science*, 18(5), 2007, pp. 429–434. — The household-energy field study showing the descriptive-norm boomerang (below-average users increasing consumption) and its correction by an injunctive signal (beat 7).
- Sherif, Muzafer. *The Psychology of Social Norms*. Harper & Brothers, 1936. — The autokinetic-effect convergence studies; ambiguity-driven adoption of a group standard (cross-referenced, item 36).
- Asch, Solomon E. "Opinions and Social Pressure." *Scientific American*, 193(5), 1955, pp. 31–35. — The line-judgment conformity experiments; echoing a majority against the evidence of one's own eyes (cross-referenced, item 35).
