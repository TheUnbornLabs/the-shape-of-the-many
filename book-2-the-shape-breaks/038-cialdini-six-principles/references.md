# References — 38. Cialdini's six principles of influence

## Confirmed sources

- Cialdini, Robert B. *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion*. Harper & Row / William Morrow, 1984 (rev. eds. subsequent). — The primary source: the six principles (reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity), the "compliance professionals" participant-observation method, the "click, whirr" fixed-action-pattern framing, and the Hare Krishna flower anecdote.
- Cialdini, Robert B. *Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade*. Simon & Schuster, 2016. — Introduces/formalises the seventh principle, unity (shared identity); later editions of *Influence* incorporate it as a seventh principle.
- Regan, Dennis T. "Effects of a favor and liking on compliance." *Journal of Experimental Social Psychology*, 7(6), 1971, 627–639. — Controlled reciprocity experiment: an unsolicited favour (buying a soft drink) increased subsequent compliance (raffle-ticket purchases).
- Freedman, Jonathan L., and Scott C. Fraser. "Compliance without pressure: The foot-in-the-door technique." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 4(2), 1966, 195–202. — Foundational commitment-and-consistency study; a small initial agreement raises compliance with a larger later request.
- Milgram, Stanley. *Obedience to Authority: An Experimental View*. Harper & Row, 1974. — Underpins the authority principle and the cross-reference to item 37.
