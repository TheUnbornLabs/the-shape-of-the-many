# References — 27. Availability cascades

## Confirmed sources

- Kuran, Timur, and Cass R. Sunstein. "Availability Cascades and Risk Regulation." *Stanford Law Review* 51, no. 4 (1999): 683–768. — The origin of the term and theory; the two-loop (informational + reputational) model, availability entrepreneurs, and the risk-regulation argument that a society mobilises against small vivid risks while ignoring large quiet ones. Anchors beats 1, 2, 3, 6, and 7.

- Tversky, Amos, and Daniel Kahneman. "Availability: A Heuristic for Judging Frequency and Probability." *Cognitive Psychology* 5, no. 2 (1973): 207–232. — The availability heuristic: people judge frequency and probability by the ease with which instances come to mind. The individual-level bias on which the social cascade is built. Supports beats 1, 2, and 3.
