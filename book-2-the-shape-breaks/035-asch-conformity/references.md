# References — 35. Asch's conformity experiments

## Confirmed sources

- Asch, Solomon E. "Effects of group pressure upon the modification and distortion of judgments." In Harold Guetzkow (ed.), *Groups, Leadership and Men: Research in Human Relations*. Carnegie Press, 1951, pp. 177–190. — The first published report of the line-judgment conformity experiments; establishes the confederate-and-standard-line design.
- Asch, Solomon E. "Opinions and Social Pressure." *Scientific American* 193(5), 1955, pp. 31–35. — The widely read general-audience account; source of the popular framing and of the single-dissenting-ally ("true partner") result.
- Asch, Solomon E. "Studies of independence and conformity: I. A minority of one against a unanimous majority." *Psychological Monographs: General and Applied* 70(9), 1956, pp. 1–70 (Whole No. 416). — The fullest technical account; source for the ~one-third average conformity on critical trials, the ~three-quarters of participants conforming at least once, the sub-1% control error rate, and the variations on majority size and unanimity.
- Bond, Rod, and Peter B. Smith. "Culture and conformity: A meta-analysis of studies using Asch's (1952b, 1956) line judgment task." *Psychological Bulletin* 119(1), 1996, pp. 111–137. — Cross-cultural meta-analysis across 17 countries; documents that conformity varies with individualism/collectivism and has declined in the US over time. Supports the WEIRD and era-bound critiques.

## Further reading to verify before final publication

- Exact conformity percentages web-verified: ~75% of participants conformed on at least one critical trial; ~32% average conformity across the 12 critical trials (commonly cited in the 32–37% range depending on condition); control-condition error rate below 1%. The chapter's figures are accurate. (Sources concur with Asch 1956, Psychological Monographs.)
- The claim that the single-ally condition reduced conformity "by around three-quarters" — verify the exact reduction figure against Asch 1955/1956; magnitude is well attested but the precise fraction should be pinned.
- The detail that an ally giving a *different* wrong answer (dissenting from the majority without giving the correct answer) still reduced conformity — attributed to Asch's variations; confirm exact condition and wording before final publication.
- The characterization of the 1950s US as an unusually "conformist era" and the observed decline in US conformity over time rests on Bond & Smith's 1996 analysis and secondary discussion; verify the direction and strength of the temporal trend as stated there.
- Description of Asch's films showing participant discomfort (pauses, squinting, nervous laughter): widely reported and consistent with the filmed record, but the vivid behavioral details are a composite illustration; confirm against primary footage/descriptions before treating any single gesture as documented.
- Sample composition ("overwhelmingly young American men, mostly college students"): consistent with the Swarthmore-era studies; confirm exact demographics from Asch 1956 before final publication.
