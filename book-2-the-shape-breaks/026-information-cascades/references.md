# References — 26. Information cascades

## Confirmed sources

- Bikhchandani, Sushil; Hirshleifer, David; Welch, Ivo. "A Theory of Fads, Fashion, Custom, and Cultural Change as Informational Cascades." *Journal of Political Economy*, vol. 100, no. 5, 1992, pp. 992–1026. — The founding paper; establishes that rational Bayesian agents deciding in sequence will abandon private signals and herd. Source for the mechanism, the "starved information" property, and the fragility claim.
- Banerjee, Abhijit V. "A Simple Model of Herd Behavior." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, vol. 107, no. 3, 1992, pp. 797–817. — The independent, contemporaneous formalization of rational herding; cited as the second 1992 arrival that reached the same result by a different route.
- Anderson, Lisa R.; Holt, Charles A. "Information Cascades in the Laboratory." *American Economic Review*, vol. 87, no. 5, 1997, pp. 847–862. — The canonical urn/ball experiment; supports the claim that real subjects fall into cascades (including incorrect ones) at rates close to the Bayesian prediction.
- Bikhchandani, Sushil; Hirshleifer, David; Welch, Ivo. "Learning from the Behavior of Others: Conformity, Fads, and Informational Cascades." *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, vol. 12, no. 3, 1998, pp. 151–170. — The review essay that carried the framework into fashion, finance, politics, and law; supports the wider-application discussion in beats 2 and 6 and the distinction between informational and normative herding in the critique.
